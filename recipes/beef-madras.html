Beef Madras

Description

If replacing beef, consider lamb. If using a 'white' meat, consider whether the meat will get more tough if cooked for longer.

This recipe describes using a heavy pot which will be transferred to the oven. It is possible to not need the oven, and cook on the stove top.

Ingredients (4 portions)

  • 600g of diced beef
  • 2 to 4 bay leaves
  • 1 tbsp of madras curry powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 large onion - grated
  • 1 large carrot - grated (optional)
  • 1 thumb size piece of ginger - grated
  • 2 whole red chillies, halved (deseeded if desired)
  • 3 cloves of garlic - crushed
  • 1 tsp ground corriander
  • 500ml passata
  • 500 ml beef stock
  • 2 tbsp ghee (or oil)

Steps

  1. Allow beef to come to room temparature, and season well.
  2. Whilst oven is pre-heating, add a small amount of oil to a heavy pot and brown off the beef in batches on a medium to high heat.
  3. Set beef aside, retain any 'juices' for the stock, and add 1 tbsp oil to the pot and turn heat to low and allow it to heat gently.
  4. Once the oil is warmed, add the grated onion, carrot, ginger, and garlic, and sweat the contents.
  5. At this stage pre-heat the oven (if using) to 140c (fan) or 160c (conventional).
  6. As the ingredients start to soften and the sugars are released, add the chillis and turn up the heat a small amount.
  7. Continue to cook for a few minutes, stirring continuously, then add ground corriander and the madras powder.
  8. If the mixture is dry add a splash of water and coat all ingredients in the spices.
  9. Add the last tbsp of ghee or oil and reintroduce the beef, coating it well.
  10. Once the beef is coated, add all other ingredients (stock, passata, bay leaves) and turn up the heat.
  11. Once the mixture is just ready to 'bubble', take off the heat, cover and place in the oven.
  12. Cook for at least 45 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken.
  13. Serve as intended, garnished with fresh corriander and chilli slices.
